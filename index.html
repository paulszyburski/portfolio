Training pipeline

From driving logic
to learned behavior.

The scripted controller drives first. Its steering and speed commands become the training data.

01

Randomize

Spawn the vehicle at different positions and orientations around the parking target.

02

Observe

Read position, heading, velocity, steering angle, target error and obstacle distances.

03

Drive expertly

Run the scripted parking controller and calculate the correct speed and steering command.

04

Record examples

Store state/action pairs across many successful simulation episodes.

05

Train the network

Fit a neural model that predicts the expert action from the current vehicle state.

Engineering decision

Why not start
with reinforcement learning?

I already had a scripted controller that could park the car. Using it as a teacher gave me useful data quickly and made mistakes easier to inspect.

Known training examples

Each example comes from a controller with clear parking rules.

Easy to regenerate

I can create a new dataset after changing the environment or parking logic.

Easier debugging

Inputs, predictions, collisions and results can be compared directly.

Reinforcement learning later

The trained model could later be used as a starting point for reinforcement learning.

What I built

Main parts of the project

01

Simulation environment

Defined the vehicle, steering joints, obstacles, collision checks, camera behavior and reset logic.

Result

Repeatable episodes with changing start positions and measurable success conditions.

02

Dataset generator

Created a scripted controller that approaches the target, aligns the car and records every decision.

Result

Structured examples containing state features and the correct control action.

03

Model evaluation

Tracked successful episodes, collisions and step limits instead of judging the model from one visual run.

Result

A repeatable way to measure whether learned behavior actually improved.

Engineering focus

The real world adds
new problems.

01

Reliable power

Computers, motors and sensors need different voltages and can create noisy loads.

Approach

Test power conversion and distribution separately before connecting the complete robot.

02

Noisy measurements

Real sensors vary with angle, lighting, surfaces and movement.

Approach

Repeat readings, validate them and build control logic that expects imperfect data.

03

Coordinate transfer

Simulation exposes exact positions while physical hardware only sees indirect measurements.

Approach

Estimate local position from camera angles and measured distances, then correct after every movement.

The product

What the app does

Reptra was designed around the moment that matters most: being in the gym and needing to record a set immediately. The interface keeps the current exercise, weight, reps and completed sets visible without forcing the user through unnecessary screens.

Outside the active workout, the app turns training history into useful views. A calendar, muscle distribution chart and body overview make consistency and balance easier to understand while routines keep repeat sessions quick to start.

Product design

The design uses high contrast, large touch targets and a small number of strong accent colors. Important workout actions are blue, completed sets are green, and supporting information stays visually quieter so the user always knows what to do next.

The idea

What Goldbridge does

Goldbridge starts with raw offer data collected from different sources. That data can be inconsistent, incomplete and difficult to compare, so the first job is to normalize it into a predictable structure that every later stage can understand.

Instead of producing a single unexplained score, the system records why a candidate passed or failed. That makes the output useful for analysis, debugging and future improvements, not just for one automated run.

Engineering focus

Problems I had to solve

01

Messy input

Different sources describe the same kind of information in completely different ways.

Solution

Normalize every record early and keep the schema consistent throughout the pipeline.

02

Weak search intent

A query can sound relevant while still attracting people who are unlikely to buy.

Solution

Store intent signals, search evidence and validation reasons instead of relying on one guess.

03

Black-box filtering

Deleting failed candidates makes it difficult to understand and improve the system.

Solution

Preserve rejected records together with the exact reason they did not pass.
